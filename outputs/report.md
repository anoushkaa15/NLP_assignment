The article demonstrates a moderate to high level of credibility by presenting a balanced perspective on the city's new climate resilience plan. It includes diverse viewpoints from officials, supporters, critics, and affected residents, and incorporates specific quantitative data. The reporting style is primarily informative, with low indicators of overt political bias or sensationalism, though it effectively conveys the urgency and public sentiment surrounding the issue.

*   The tone is informative and balanced, effectively conveying an underlying urgency related to heavy flooding and public frustration without resorting to sensationalism.

*   Low indicators of overt political bias or sensationalism were identified, suggesting a neutral reporting stance.

*   Cross-reference the information with official municipal documents, independent expert analyses, or other news outlets for a comprehensive understanding.
*   Monitor future reports on the implementation progress of the climate resilience plan and ongoing public feedback.

*   The 'attribution quality' was assessed as 'moderate', suggesting there might be room for even more detailed sourcing in some areas.
